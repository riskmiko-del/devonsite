2. Outdoor Activities — Specific Risks

Wild Swimming

  • Wild swimming in rivers, lakes, reservoirs, the sea and tidal pools carries serious risks including cold water shock, drowning, unseen currents, underwater hazards, contaminated water and rapidly changing conditions.
  • Never swim alone. Always check water quality, river levels and weather forecasts before entering the water.
  • The presence of a location on this website is not a recommendation that it is safe to swim at any particular time.
  • Children must be actively supervised at all times near water.

Surfing & Watersports

  • Surfing, bodyboarding, paddleboarding and other watersports carry risks including injury from boards, rocks and other water users, rip currents, submersion and cold water shock.
  • Always use appropriate equipment, check surf conditions and wave heights before entering the water, and follow the advice of RNLI lifeguards where present.
  • Beginners should take professional lessons from a qualified surf school before surfing independently.

Coastal & Moorland Walking

  • Coastal paths, cliff edges and moorland walks carry risks including unstable ground, sudden cliff collapse, fast-changing weather, mist and disorientation, and remote locations with no mobile signal.
  • Always carry an OS map or appropriate navigation device, wear suitable footwear, carry water and food, and tell someone your planned route and return time.
  • Keep a safe distance from cliff edges at all times. Do not approach the cliff edge to take photographs.
  • On Dartmoor, be aware of military firing ranges. Check range closure times at dartmoor.gov.uk before visiting remote areas.

Camping & Wild Camping

  • Wild camping on Dartmoor is permitted under the Dartmoor Commons Act but campers must follow the Dartmoor Camping Code. Elsewhere in England, wild camping requires landowner permission.
  • Open fires may be prohibited, especially during dry periods. Always check local fire restrictions. Never leave fires unattended.
  • Campers are responsible for their own safety, including awareness of weather, wildlife and their equipment.

Rock Pooling & Beach Activities

  • Be aware of incoming tides when rock pooling — always check tide times before visiting rocky shores and never allow the tide to cut off your return route.
  • Wet rocks and seaweed are extremely slippery. Wear appropriate footwear and move carefully.

5. Emergency Information

In an emergency in the UK, call 999. For coastal and sea emergencies, call 999 and ask for the Coastguard. For non-emergency coastguard assistance, call 01803 882704 (Brixham MRCC).

The RNLI operates lifeguarded beaches across Devon from May to September. Always swim between the red and yellow flags where lifeguards are present.
